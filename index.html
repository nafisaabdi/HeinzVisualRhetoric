Visual Rhetoric

Rhetoric is the “the ability to see the available means of persuasion in each case” (Aristotle 1991). Visual rhetoric relative to the traditional oral view of rhetoric can be considered a somewhat recent development. Visual rhetoric provides us with a lens to analyze the images we view on a regular basis. We are able to explain the ways in which images communicate meaning to the audience.Understanding visual rhetoric allows us to use it to our advantage. We are able to understand how to convey our message in the most efficient manner.

In order to successfully deliver the message, it is vital that everything is intentionally selected. This is facilitated by our use of colors, typography, and layout they all assist us in delivering a message. Even the absence of color and text conveys a message to the viewer. With this understanding, we are able to recognize that visual rhetoric is found everywhere. Rhetoric “is more broadly about the ways humans communicate with one another and how communication can shape human understanding of and decision making about ecosystems.” (Druschke and McGreavy) It is important to understand that visual rhetoric doesn’t just persuade, but it also conveys information.
